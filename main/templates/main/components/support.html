Need help reading a scan or improving a finding?

Use this page as a quick guide for understanding Darklake results, preparing a target, and deciding what to do after a passive analysis.

Before scanning

Use a public website URL

Darklake works with public HTTP and HTTPS targets. Internal addresses, private IPs, and authenticated-only areas are outside the passive scan scope.

Results

Reports

FAQ

Does Darklake perform attacks?

No. It performs passive analysis of public signals and does not exploit, brute force, or authenticate.

Why can results differ from a full audit?

Passive checks can only see externally visible signals. Hidden application logic needs deeper testing.

What should I do after a high-risk result?

Confirm the evidence, apply the recommendation, and rerun the analysis after the change is deployed.
